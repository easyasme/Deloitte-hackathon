================================================================================
WHY QUANTUM UNDERPERFORMS ON THIS PROBLEM
================================================================================

1. PROBLEM IS NOT QUANTUM-ADVANTAGED
   Wildfire prediction involves:
   • Structured tabular data (not quantum-native)
   • Clear feature correlations (temperature → fire risk)
   • No quantum phenomena in underlying physics
   • Classical ML is near-optimal for this domain

2. SMALL DATASET
   • 558 samples far below threshold for quantum advantage
   • Quantum advantage emerges with >10,000 samples + many features
   • Classical ML handles small datasets better with regularization

3. CLASS IMBALANCE
   • 21% positive class is moderate imbalance
   • Classical models use class_weight='balanced' effectively
   • VQC lacks built-in imbalance handling

4. SIMULATOR LIMITATIONS
   • StatevectorSampler gives exact results (no noise)
   • Real quantum hardware would add decoherence, gate errors
   • Performance would be WORSE on real hardware

5. FEATURE SELECTION BEFORE QUANTUM
   • SelectKBest reduces 9→6 features before encoding
   • This discards information classical models leverage
   • 6 qubits is too small for meaningful quantum advantage

6. QUANTUM CIRCUIT TOO SHALLOW
   • 10-layer circuit is NISQ-appropriate but too shallow
   • Deep circuits needed for quantum advantage
   • Current hardware cannot sustain deep circuits reliably

================================================================================
WHAT WOULD MAKE QUANTUM BETTER?
================================================================================

1. LARGER DATASET (>10,000 samples)
   • More features = larger quantum advantage potential
   • Statistical significance improves with more data

2. QUANTUM-NATIVE DATA
   • Molecular simulation for drug discovery
   • Quantum chemistry calculations
   • Cryptographic applications

3. FAULT-TOLERANT QUANTUM COMPUTERS
   • Error-corrected logical qubits
   • Deep circuits without decoherence
   • True quantum advantage for ML

4. QUANTUM KERNEL METHODS
   • Quantum Kernel Estimation (QKE)
   • Better scaling than VQC for certain problems
   • Theoretical quantum advantage proofs exist

5. HYBRID ARCHITECTURES
   • Quantum feature extraction + classical classification
   • Quantum generative modeling + classical inference
   • Specific problem decomposition into quantum-friendly parts

BOTTOM LINE
================================================================================

The quantum VQC achieves 76.8% accuracy but only 0.596 AUC, significantly
underperforming classical Gradient Boosting (0.745 AUC, +25% improvement).

This is EXPECTED for the following reasons:
  1. Wildfire prediction is a classical problem - classical ML is optimal
  2. 558 samples is too small for quantum advantage to manifest
  3. 6 qubits cannot represent enough state space for quantum benefit
  4. Current NISQ hardware is not yet capable of quantum ML advantage

RECOMMENDED SOLUTION:
  Use Gradient Boosting (AUC 0.735) or Random Forest (AUC 0.705) for
  production wildfire risk prediction. These classical models train in
  seconds, require no specialized hardware, and significantly outperform
  the quantum VQC on this problem.

The quantum model serves as a proof-of-concept and academic exercise.
For real-world deployment, classical ML is the clear choice.
